    How Should Christians Approach Progress in Technology?

    March 9, 2024 · 4 min

    The Christian approach to technology should be well-thought-out and balanced. This means that we must consider both the benefits of technological advancements and the potential challenges they bring. In this article, we will discuss how Christians can navigate and approach progress in technology with wisdom and discernment. We will explore several key ideas including understanding God's creative nature, evaluating the impact on humanity, being aware of potential dangers or consequences, maintaining balance in our lives, using technology for God's glory, and cultivating humility as we engage with new developments.

    Understanding God’s Creative Nature

    As believers, we understand that God is the originator of all creativity and innovation (Genesis 1:1). Christians believe that God created everything, including the natural world and mankind. As we observe the progression of technology, we recognize God's handiwork in these inventions and discoveries. Our appreciation for His divine nature helps us maintain a proper perspective regarding technology.

    While acknowledging God's creative nature, we also recognize that humans possess an inherent drive to create, innovate, and improve their circumstances. These endeavors often include advancements in technology. Thus, instead of fearing technology per se, Christians should view it as an expression of God's image within human beings (Genesis 1:26-27).

    Evaluating the Impact on Humanity

    As followers of Christ, we should assess the influence of technological developments on humanity holistically. Technological progress has led to numerous advancements, such as modern medicine, better communication tools, and improved transportation methods. However, we must also acknowledge potential detriments such as increased isolation or loss of privacy. It's vital to approach technology critically yet remain open to its benefits.

    We do well by considering the following questions when evaluating new technologies:

    • How does this innovation benefit humanity overall?
    • Does this development have any negative effects? If so, how can these potential issues be addressed or mitigated?
    • What are the ethical implications associated with this technology? Are there ways we can ensure responsible use?
    • Will engaging with this technology distract us from priorities like family relationships, spiritual growth, or time spent in prayer?

    Being Aware of Potential Dangers or Consequences

    While many technologies greatly enhance our quality of life, we need to be mindful of unintended consequences or dangers they may pose. For example, though social media platforms enable widespread connection, they can also contribute to cyberbullying, misinformation spread, or addiction (Matthew 6:24).

    Developing wisdom in navigating technology requires being informed about current challenges and risks as well as staying proactive concerning future consequences. This includes educating ourselves about privacy concerns surrounding newer tech devices and staying vigilant about possible deceptions or exploitation online. Additionally, Christians should promote discusions within communities about responsible tech usage. By doing so, we uphold biblical principles while responsibly engaging with the world around us.

    Maintaining Balance in Our Lives

    A significant challenge in today's society is striking the right balance between embracing technological advancement and preserving essential aspects of our daily lives—such as meaningful interpersonal interactions, outdoor activities, restorative sleep patterns, or personal hygiene practices (Hebrews 12:1). As believers, we must remember that "everything created...should be received with thanksgiving"—yet always employed responsibly (1 Timothy 4:4).

    Ultimately, we should utilize technology as a tool but not let it define our existence or overshadow other critical facets of life. We can preserve balance by regularly examining how we incorporate technology into our routines, ensuring it serves a purpose rather than becoming an end unto itself.

    Using Technology for God’s Glory

    As disciples of Jesus Christ, we seek to please Him in every aspect of life, including our engagement with technology. The apostle Paul urges believers to “whatever you do, do it all for the glory of God” (1 Corinthians 10:31). Christians must aspire to harness technology in ways that glorify God.

    This involves using digital platforms to share the Good News, show compassion towards others through online counseling initiatives, or even employ technology as a tool in ministry endeavors (Romans 15:13). Reframing technological usage as a means toward God's glory brings purpose and meaning to our interactions with technology—truly aligning with our Christian worldview.

    Cultivating Humility as We Engage with New Developments

    Technological advancements evolve at rapid speeds, making it challenging for anyone—even experts—to stay updated on every development. As Christians, we must maintain humility as we venture into unfamiliar territory. Avoid becoming overly confident or arrogant about our knowledge base. Rather, we ought to pursue continued learning alongside a posture of teachability. Embracing humility allows us to approach technology with caution and discernment while still reaping its benefits. It reminds us of our dependence upon God's wisdom, even amidst the complexities of scientific progress.

    In conclusion, Christians should navigate technological progress thoughtfully and with faithfulness to their beliefs. By considering God's creative nature, evaluating potential impacts, remaining attentive to dangers or consequences, prioritizing balance in our lives, utilizing technology for God's glory, and cultivating humility as we explore new developments—we demonstrate wisdom and maturiyy in embracing the gifts technology provides while minimizing its hazards. In this way, we grow in our walk with Christ while positively influencing a culture heavily shaped by technological advancements.
