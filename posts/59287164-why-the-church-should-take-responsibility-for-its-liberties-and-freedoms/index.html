    Why the Church Should Take Responsibility for Its Liberties and Freedoms

    March 2, 2024 · 4 min
    Table of Contents
    • A Historical Context: Preventing State Persecution & Protecting Doctrine
    • Balancing Spiritual Freedom With Social Responsibility
    • Christian Love Expressed Through Constructive Engagement

    In America, as in many other countries where religious liberty is enshrined in law or practice, there remains a kind of tension between government—particularly those actors charged with upholding secular laws—and religious bodies. While the tensions can manifest themselves in different ways (e.g., funding disputes between faith-based schools and their local authorities), one primary area of conflict seems to hinge upon how religious bodies understand their own responsibilities for both preserving their own liberties and freedoms while not encroaching on those same liberties for others. In short, there must be a delicate balance between asserting religious claims without imposing them unilaterally on society at large. As such, it falls to the Church itself—its leaders and adherents alike—to thoughtfully consider how the various freedoms they enjoy should be pursued in light of mutual respect for others and love for neighbor. In this essay I hope to lay out the reasons why the Church should make this consideration its top priority rather than responding defensively when challenged by the state or by secularists concerning perceived infringements on its liberties.

    A Historical Context: Preventing State Persecution & Protecting Doctrine

    The call for churches to take responsibility for their liberties has deep historical roots arising from centuries of persecutions visited upon Christians at the hands of state actors throughout Europe during the Roman era right through until modern times. These tragedies stand as stark reminders that governments can target religious communities deemed threatening by political leadership. The atrocities perpetuated against Jews during World War II similarly serve as dark lessons about the potential dangers inherent within authoritarian regimes. Recognizing these realities, religious communities have appropriately embraced concepts like religious toleration and freedom as safeguards against oppression. This defense often requires conscious engagement with legal structures so as to promote both the internal flourishing of the Church while ensuring outsider protection from the state’s misuse of power over religious matters.

    However, it would be equally important to avoid the opposite extreme: an exclusively defensive posture that compromises authentic witness in favor solely of self-preservation strategies. Such hesitation might undermine certain commitments held dear by a people of faith that led them into engagement with societal challenges initially. If mere survival becomes the raison d'être for religious communities, theology can slip away into irrelevance. The legacy of prophetic challenge to social norms becomes obfuscated behind shields of legal accommodationism. This is when a faith community slowly loses sight of its mission to transform the world in light of God's promises.

    Balancing Spiritual Freedom With Social Responsibility

    Religious freedom, as enshrined in many constitutions worldwide, ensures individual freedom to maintain beliefs as well as collective rights for practices congruent with those convictions. However, it does not grant absolute license for any act derived from one’s deeply held convictions. Herein lies part of the difficulty: recognizing legitimate boundaries where personal beliefs impact broader society necessitates accountability without succumbing to capitulation. It means finding ways of engaging culture while remaining true to principle - an ongoing challenge for faith communities that require care and wisdom.

    This balancing act does not absolve religious institutions from their spiritual mandate; rather, it highlights the responsibility of actively considering how doctrine translates into action. When faith traditions are transparent regarding the underlying motives behind their practices, they allow room for reasoned discussion with those outside their ranks. Where transparency falters, unnecessary resentment grows among secularized citizens who may perceive encroachment upon their own freedoms—even if those concerns are somewhat exaggerated or misguided. In fact, refusing opportunities for conversation between religious adherents and non-believers can itself be seen as an abdication of our religious duty to share truth as loving witnesses within fallen worlds.

    Christian Love Expressed Through Constructive Engagement

    At heart lies a core Christian commitment—love your neighbor as yourself—which serves as a powerful guiding principle for churches wishing to navigate these complex waters. Fostering dialogue with civil authorities and citizens at large helps illustrate genuine concern for common goods above narrow sectarian interests. Acting cooperatively towards building stronger communities transcends mere tolerance into something approaching mutual appreciation. By patiently investing time and effort in nurturing communal bonds, faith groups demonstrate concretely how their liberties are exercised in solidarity with all those created in God’s image regardless of belief systems.

    To conclude then, churches should take responsibility for their liberties but with an emphasis on embracing constructive engagement rather than simply defending their privileges. By working towards open dialogue across differing viewpoints and cultivating relationships based on understanding rather than suspicion, Christianity demonstrates both its dedication to scriptural principles as well as its commitment to securing religious freedom for all, including those who don't share its doctrinal tenets. It is through humble collaboration with others that the radiance of Christ's love shines most brightly in a pluralistic world, modeling a manner of living befitting subjects of Heaven's King who rule not by force but rather through gentle persuasion grounded in sacrificial service.
